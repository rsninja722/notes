## true false

- A balance sheet is a required financial statement that reports the financial position of the company for a **point** of time
- Gains are different from revenues because they are from different sources, but affect the financial statement the same way
    - revenues result from ongoing business operations
    - gains result from peripheral operations
- Liabilities are economic obligations to creditors to be paid at some future date by the company
- The primary objective of financial reporting is to provide information useful for making investment and lending decisions
- Outside investors would ordinarily use financial accounting information to decide whether to invest in a business
- Although the external users of financial accounting statements have different needs for accounting information, accountants prepare only one set of financial statements for all users
