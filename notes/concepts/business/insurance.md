# insurance (life, health, disability, property, home, and automobile)

## nature of insurance

paying for another party to face your financial consequences in the event something happens

- **risk pooling/risk sharing** - each member contributes an amount, members who suffer collect from the pool, members who suffer no loss lose the amount
    - paying a smaller set amount to avoid a potential larger amount

- **premium** - amount a member must pay in
    - depends on the probability of the occurrence for that member, and the payout amount

- two conditions required for functioning insurance
    - **adverse selection** - people who join are most likely to suffer
        - need members who are unlikely to suffer to keep premiums down
        - balanced pool required
    - **moral hazard** - a party having a tendency to take risk because the costs incurred would not be felt by the risk taking party
        - must be avoided
        - must be a financial incentive to behave responsibly

# life and house insurance

## life insurance

- **insured** - person whose death will trigger payment of death benefit
- **beneficiary** - who receives the benefit
- **death benefit/face value** - dollar amount paid to beneficiary if insured dies
- **owner** - person who pays for policy 
    - tends to be same as insured
- **policy term** - period policy is in force
- **rate** - cost of each unit of insurance
- **insurability** - qualification for buying the policy (ex. medical exam)
    - goal for company: minimize adverse selection problem
