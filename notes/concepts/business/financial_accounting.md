# financial accounting

## equations




- **balance** - beginning balance​ + issuance of shares​ - dividends​ + net income​ (or - net​ loss) = ending balance
- **equity** - ending equity = beginning equity + change in capital + net income - dividend
- **ending cash​** = beginning cash​ + collections​ - payments
- **collections​** = sales​ - amount not collected
- **payments​** = inventory payment​ + all other payments
- **notes payable​** = beginning notes payable​ + borrowed notes payable​ - paid off notes payable
- **amount stolen​** = adjusted book balance​ - adjusted bank balance
- **adjusted bank balance​** = bank statement balance​ + deposits in transit​ - outstanding cheques
- **book balance of cash​** = adjusted bank balance​ + service charge​ - interest earned​ + NSF cheque​ - book error

- **ASPE**
    - **consulting price per hour​** = total contract​ ÷ months in year​ ÷ hours per month
- **IFRS**
    - **hours per year​** = hours per month​ × months in year
    - **consulting price per hour**​ = total contract​ ÷ obligation hours

## definitions

- **accounting** - an information system that measures and records business activities, and processes data into reports
    - "the language of business"
    - must be complete, neutral, without error and record events that result in change in assets, liabilities, or equity
    - **financial accounting** - provides information for everyone inside and outside of the business
        - what is usually meant when people just say "accounting"
        - provides information to investors, creditors, government, non-profits, customers, suppliers, employees, and unions
        - information is specifically prepared for investors and creditors
    - **management accounting** - generates inside information for the managers (or internal members) of the organization 
- **accrued liability** - expense incurred but not yet payed
- **accrual basis** - record expenses/revenue when transactions occur
- **accrued expense** - expense incurred but not yet payed by the business
- **bookkeeping** - the act of recording and manipulating data for accounting
- **capital** - funds contributed by owners
- **cash basis** - record expenses/revenue when payment is received/disbursed
- **dividends declared** - when a corporation decides to distribute dividends
    - declared before actually payed
- **dividends payable** - liability resulting from declared dividends that aren't yet payed
- **dividends revenue** - dividends earned on investment in other stocks
- **deferred revenue** - advanced payments for a good/service to be payed in the future
- **funding**
    - money to support the business
    - provided by investors and creditors, with the hope of returns
- **gains** - money from peripheral operations
- **owners' equity** - portion of the business assets owned outright by the owners
- **revenues** - money from ongoing business operations
- **shareholder** - person/entity invested in a company in exchange for a share of ownership
    - shareholders have ultimate control of a company
- **shareholder's equity** - value of the company's obligation to shareholders / net worth of company
- **share capital** - money invested in the company through issuing stocks/shares

### assumptions

- **going-concern assumption** - an organization will continue operating normally for the forseeable future
- **historical-cost assumption** - assets should be recorded at their actual cost, at the date of purchase
- **separate-entity assumption** - business activities are separate from the activities of its owner(s) / other divisions of the business
- **stable-money assumption** - financial information is reported assuming the value of the currency is stable 

### standards

- **ASPE** - Accounting Standards for Private Enterprises
- **GAAP** - generally accepted accounting principals
    - established by **CPAC** (Chartered Professional Accountants of Canada​)
- **IFRS** - international financial reporting standards
    - must be used by organizations listed on TSX

## important points

- recognize revenues when they are earned, not when collected
- recognize expenses when incurred, not when paid
- received capital is not income
- investment in company - debit assets, credit common shares
- expenses decrease shareholder equity
- good ratios: high current ratio and low debt ratio

## financial statements

- **financial statements** - business documents that companies use to report the results of their activities to various user groups, financial standing, and performance
    - primary objective: provide useful info for investing and lending decisions


the following are in order:

### trial balance

- shows closing balance of all accounts

- trial balance list all accounts in the following order:
    - assets - current and long term, ordered by liquidity (closeness to conversion by cash)
        - **assets** - represent rights to future benefits
        - **current assets** - future benefits realized within a year
            - cash, accounts receivable, inventory, prepaid insurance, prepaid rent
        - **future assets** - future benefits realized over a period greater than a year
            - equipment, 
    - liabilities - current and long term, ordered by maturity
        - **liabilities** - obligations to give future benefits
        - **current liabilities** - due/mature within a year
            - accounts payable, advertising payable, income tax payable, dividends payable
            - unearned revenue - collects cash from customers, promising to provide services in the future
        - **future liabilities** - due/mature in more than a year
            - accumulated deprecation - equipment,
    - equity
    - dividends
    - revenues and gains
    - expenses and losses

- header - name,trial balance, December 31 XXXX

### income statement

- reports the revenues and expenses for a specific period     
- header - name,income statement, for the year ended December 31 XXXX

- **net income** = total income - total expenses
    - **total income** = revenues + gains
    - **total expenses** = expenses + losses


### statement of retained earnings

- wealth increase
- header - name,statement of retained earnings, for the year ended December 31 XXXX

- **ending retained earnings** = beginning returned earnings + net income - dividends

### balance sheet

- reports the​ assets, liabilities, and​ owners' equity of the entity at a particular point in time.
- header - name,balance sheet, as at date
- **classified balance sheet** - separates current/non-current assets/liabilities

- **accounting equation** - assets = liabilities + owner's equity
- assets = property + equipment + cash + receivables + prepaid expenses + other assets
- liabilities = long term liabilities + current liabilities
- equity = common shares (capital) + retained earnings

### statement of cash flow

- cash generated and spent
- most important section: operating activities

- **increase in cash** = operating cash flows + investing cash flows + financing cash flows
