## financial intermediary

- main 4 types of institutions: banks, trust companies, life insurance companies, and investment dealers

- **banks**
    - hold idle cash, put it to use through loans and mortgages 
    - often only possible through economies of scale and scope (needed to enable moving money)
    - **chartered banks**
        - schedule I - full service
        - schedule II - subsidiaries of foreign banks
            - restrictions on asset growth/lending activities
- **trust companies** - third party to manage assets of another party (example: pension managed by a trust company)
    - provides extra security against risk by protecting assets from seizures
- **credit unions** - user owned, "non-profit", cooperative
- **non-deposit type institutions** - TODO
- **insurance companies**
    - life insurance
    - property insurance
        - auto
        - home
- **investment/asset management companies**
    - pool money together and invest it for clients
- **mortgage and loan companies** - 
    - prime/sub-prime clients
        - prime mortgages reserved for reliable clients
- **finance and leasing companies** - alternative for customers that are "sub-prime" clients
- **pawn shops** - provide liquidity for tangible assets
    - creates loans or provides cash for items
- **payday loan companies** - provide short term loans at high interest rates
    - high APR (annual percentage rate)
