### stocks

- **primary market** - new issues
    - **IPO** (initial public offering)
    - offered by **underwriters/investment bankers**
        - hires accounting firm to audit a company for disclosure when registering for an IPO
        - firm issues shares to underwriters, underwriters sell to investors
        - tend to charge 5-10%
    - price tends to increase rapidly right after release
- **secondary**
    - **exchanges** (NYSE, TSX)
    - **dealers** (market makers) - provide liquidity
        - own stocks, buy from sellers, sell to buyers
        - **specialists** - all orders of a specific stock go through a specific dealer
            - benefit - all investors know they are getting the best price (price discovery)
        - **bid/ask** (insta buy/sell) - bid = dealer buy, ask = dealer sell (usually higher)
            - market sell order placed by investor gets filled at bid price
            - market buy order placed by investor gets filled at ask price
            - **spread** - difference between bid and ask (markup)
        - **limit buy/sell** (create buy/sell order)
            - buy - set maximum price willing to pay 
            - sell - set minimum price willing to sell for
            - **price priority** - best price is filled first 
            - time limits - time to leave order active
                - GTC - good till cancel
        - **stop order** - set price for trade to trigger at, trade becomes a market order upon hitting the limit
            - used to limit losses
        - settlement (money transfer) occurs the day after a trade
    - **brokers** - deal directly with individually with investors, execute trades
        - send orders to correct dealers
    - **over the counter market (OTC)** - multiple dealers offering different prices
        - NASDAQ - national association of security dealer quotation

## investment companies

- pool funds to provide exposure to diversified portfolios for small individual costs
- provide some expertise in management 

- open vs. closed ended
    - **open ended (mutual fund)** - take deposits, company manages a portfolio for a fee
        - flexible deposits (not fixed)
        - redemptions - money can be taken out at any time
        - load vs no load
            - **load** - commissions
                - **front end** - pay upfront 
                - **back end** - pay when withdrawing
                    - sliding scale - fee decreases over time
            - **no load** - 
    - **close ended** - fixed number of shares, listed on exchange
        - example: **REIT** (real estate investment trust) - fund that manages real estate and provides rental income as return

- **(NAV) Net Asset Value** = (market value of all assets - liabilities) / total units

- tax
    - **ACB (adjusted cost base)** - cost of investments including front-end load and commissions

## mutual fund fees

- management fee
- audit fee - yearly audit to ensure the holdings are accurately reported
- legal fee - for setting up the fund
- trailer fee - compensation for advisor that guides you to a certain fund
- load

all fees are conveyed together by the **MER (management expense ratio)** 

average return of canadian funds is ~4%

average return of US funds is ~5.5%

average return of US market is ~9%
