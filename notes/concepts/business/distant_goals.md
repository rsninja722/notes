# distant goals

## pension

- **pay as you go** - current payments are used to pay current retirees
    - demographic risk - the retirees requiring more than the workers can contribute
- **fully funded** - each individual has their personal contributions set aside and invested 

### public aspect

- **CPP (canadian pension plan)**
    - max benefit for 2024 = \\$1,364 per month
    - intended to provide up to 25% of pre-retirement income (to become 33% by 2065)
    - pay ~6% of YMPE into CPP every year
        - **YMPE (yearly maximum pensionable earnings)**
            - max in 2024 = \\$68500
    - based on years of contribution (up to 39.5 years)
    - employee and employer each contribute ~6%
    - time frame
        - can start withdrawing at 60, or as late as 70
        - -0.6% of benefits for every month before age 65 you start receiving
        - +0.7% of benefits for every month after 65 until 70
    - disability benefits
    - in case of death - funeral expenses and possible benefits to surviving spouse/dependant (if survivors don't have their own contributions)
- **Old Age Security (OAS)** - automatic pension benefit
    - based on years of residence in canada since 18
        - minimum of 10 years, 40 for max benefits
        - 20 years required to require outside of canada
    - included in taxable income
    - clawback - repayment required for higher income earners
        - 15% of each dollar above \\$86,912 of income is used as clawback
            - example: \\$142,609 income: (\\$142,609 - \\$86912)*0.15 = clawback = 8354.55
- **Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)** - income tested pension supplement for OAS recipients that are residents of canada
    - only for low income retirees (below ~\\$21,000)
    - 50% clawback above ~\\$4000
    - tax free
